Kayak Simulator

Back in my college days I did a bit of kayaking, so this was a bit of a passion project. My goals were to replicate the physics of a real kayak and to create an experience that was more meditative and explorative than most games I enjoy. I did most of the work just before Spartaga workload picked up, so I wasn't able to go much further with it. Maybe I'll pick it up again someday.

Fragments

Fragments was my capstone project at the University of Washington, and where I met the CEO of House of How. The goal of the project was to make a suspensful puzzle game where the game mechanics mimic symptoms of anxiety. I worked on a team of 5 where I fulfilled a general technical designer role along side 1 dev, 1 project manager, 1 researcher, and 1 designer.I worked on asset integration, puzzle/level design, gameplay programming, and research.

Space Game UI

In the early days of Spartaga I wanted to explore the new medium and in particular learn about what was newly possible with UI. This prototype was the exploration of a level select screen that leveraged the VR space. We ended up not using it because we found it easiest for the user to interact with a 2d UI in VR rather than teach them new behaviors. We did not want users getting confused with level select before the game even started, and thought it was silly to have a tutorial for the UI.
